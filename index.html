I am a 2nd year PhD student in bioinformatics, working on clonal evolution in neuroblastoma, under the supervision of Olivier DELATTRE and Gudrun SCHLEIERMACHER at U830 and Valentina BOEVA at U900. I graduated from the Ecole Polytechnique in 2014, and obtained a master's degree in Molecular and Cellular Biotherapies from Université Paris Descartes the same year. I have always enjoyed interdisciplinary work, which lead me to different topics such as quality control for Simone PERELE , scuba-diving gear handling , DNA recombination in the middle of Missouri , Human genetics in immunology, and finally programming and data analysis After some work in experimental biology at Stowers's Institute and Imagine Institute, I switched to the world of computer sciences.

However, I did not forget all of my previous work, and put it to good use, by teaching molecular and cellular biology at Université Paris Sud (1st year of Bachelor degree).

My work has focused so far on reconstructing clonal populations from whole genome sequencing data. This has lead me to develop QuantumClone, which aims to reconstruct clonal populations by statistical inference. I have also been interested in how to select the best variants to do the clustering.

R packages :

    Can be found either on CRAN or on GitHub

  • ACSNMineR

I have presented QuantumClone and its applications at ISMB/ECCB 2015 in Dublin: an oral presentation at HitSeq, and a poster both at HitSeq and ISMB

I currently am interested in data analysis, and I mainly deal with genomic data. I switched from the wet lab to full-time computer programming between my master's degree and my PhD, but I sometimes get back to the bench when giving classes at Université Paris Sud (Orsay).


Professional experience


Sept. 2014 - Now : PhD Student at Institut Curie, Paris, FRANCE


Supervised by V. BOEVA, O. DELATTRE and G. SCHLEIERMACHER

Studying clonal heterogeneity -i.e. the different populations co-existing in a tumor - in neuroblastoma, the most common solid extra-cranial cancer of childhood. Developped packages to:

  • Reconstruct the different populations based on copy number alterations and variant allele frequencies of single nucleotide variants: QuantumClone

  • Facilitate computation of over-representation of pathways based on biological networks: ACSNMineR


Sept. 2013 - June 2014 : Intern at Institut Imagine, Paris, FRANCE

Supervised by J. BUSTAMANTE

Identification and preliminary work of characterization of a disease causing variant, unit of predisposition to mycobacterial infections.

Combined immunological knowledge and experimental genetics to confirm mutations detected by Whole Exome Sequencing, and predict its function.


April. 2012 - August 2012 : Intern at Stowers Institute, Kansas City, MO, USA

Supervised by P. QIAN, laboratory of Linheng LI

Creation of a BAC-Clone for homologous recombination.

The aim of the internship was to create a bacterial artificial chromosome (BAC) that could express a cluster of miRNAs at will (TetOn promoter), that could be used for homologous recombination in mouse ES cells.
